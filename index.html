The Productivity Initiative

Empowering Egypt’s Youth At a time when productivity has become a difficult dream for many young people—due to unemployment, economic hardships, and the pressures of daily life—I decided to dedicate my graduation project to shedding light on the realities faced by Egyptian youth and to propose practical solutions that can truly be applied on the ground. This project isn’t just words on paper; it’s a sincere attempt to open a new door to understanding the challenges confronting an entire generation—and how we can overcome them with practical thinking and a hopeful spirit. By combining the Egyptian reality with German-inspired ideas—such as strategic planning, vocational training, and gradual independence—I’ve tried to build a vision that brings together both ambition and action, turning dreams into achievable steps

Real Economic and Social Independence for Egypt’s Youth

A graduation project that sheds light on the challenges facing Egyptian youth in achieving true economic and social independence—especially amid unemployment, the housing crisis, and delayed marriage. It presents solutions inspired by German thinking, adapted to fit the Egyptian context.

Who We Are

I’m a young Egyptian woman who sees how independence has become a distant dream for an entire generation. That’s why I chose to dedicate my graduation project to an issue that touches every household in Egypt: youth independence. Through field research, real social dialogues, and a deep analysis of the current reality, I’ve worked to shape a different vision—one that reflects a future worthy of us

Our Goals

• To support the idea of economic and social independence as a fundamental right.

• To raise awareness about the challenges faced by today’s youth.

• To offer practical solutions inspired by the German experience.

• To create a space for dialogue between young people and decision-makers.
